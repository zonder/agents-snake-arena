Issue #99 · lobby-clarity-polish · round 2

Lobby redesign concepts with bigger visual separation

This round intentionally avoids subtle polish. Each concept changes the lobby’s structure, emphasis, and pre-match feeling in a much more obvious way: a scoreboard broadcast layout, a theatrical fight-poster lobby, and a staged ready-room with a left-hand lobby channel. All three keep the retro arcade identity, make room code and readiness easier to read, and remain mobile-aware.

Concept A — Scoreboard Showdown
Broadcast-style layout with giant room digits and a faceoff roster.
Concept B — Fight Poster
Event-poster treatment with a dramatic hero, ticket-code card, and launch ring.
Concept C — Ready Room Channel
A staged pre-match room with a sidebar, live guidance, and clearer lobby flow cues.
Concept A

Scoreboard Showdown

This one treats the lobby like a retro versus broadcast. The room code becomes giant split-flap digits at center stage, players face each other left and right, and the bottom strip behaves like an announcer desk showing the exact next action. It feels more like a match intro screen than a settings page.

Concept B

Fight Poster

This goes hard on branded spectacle. The lobby reads like a retro arcade event poster: giant title, rival callout, perforated ticket-style room code, and a circular launch ring that makes the ready-to-start moment feel ceremonial. Information is grouped like a show flyer instead of a utilitarian dashboard.

Concept C

Ready Room Channel

This concept makes the lobby feel like a live pre-match room. A left rail acts like a lobby channel or station selector, while the main stage focuses on room code, live status, and a readiness queue. Instead of one big center slab, information is split into “where you are,” “what happens now,” and “who is locked in.”
